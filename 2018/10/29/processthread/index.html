Process : An instance of computer progam that is being executed. It is a basic unit when allocating and competing resources for program. As we all know, a program is a collection of instruction, while process is the execution of these instructions.

Tread : A thread is a smallest sequence of programmed instructions that can be managed by a scheduler. It is a basic unit when scheduling tasks for processor.

Differences

  • Processes are typically independent while threads exist as part of the process.
  • Processes have independent address spaces, while threads of a process share the same address space of the process.
  • Processes have independent resource allocation, while threads of a process share the same resorce allocation of the process.
  • Processes carry more state information than threads.
  • Switching between processes cost more than switching between threads.
  • Switching between threads is faster than switching between processes.
  • processes interact only through system-provided inter-process communication mechanisms
  • Every process has an entrence of excution, while a thread cannot execute independently.

multiprocessing and multipthreading

When should we use multiprocessing and when should we use multithreading ?

Fistly, the execution cost of threads is relatively low, while threads are weak considering managing resources. Threads are really suitable executing on Symmetrical Multi-Processing (SMP) system. When it is required to execute in a high effective and frequently switching environment, multithreading is your first choice.

Secondly, the execution cost of processes is relatively high, while processes are effective considering managing and protecting resources. When it is required to have a good management of resources without considering cost and effectiveness, multiprocessing is a good choice.
